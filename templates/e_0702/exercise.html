Determining the constant tension when the ball moves down the groove

By pressing the red button, the measurement system is reset. First, place the ball at the top of an inclined plane with a slope angle of 30°. Allow it to move freely under the force of gravity. Record the elapsed time from the initial moment to when the ball leaves the inclined plane. Perform the measurements three times. Determine the average time value as well as the absolute error in time measurement. Calculate the acceleration value a1 and determine the relative error of the experiment. Repeat the procedure for an inclined plane with a slope angle of 45°.
