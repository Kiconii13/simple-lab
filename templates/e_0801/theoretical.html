Determining the equivalent resistance of series and parallel connection of resistors

Objective of the exercise: Comparing the experimental values of the equivalent resistance of the system of 2 resistors connected in parallel or in series, with theoretical calculations.
Description of the exercise: We will perform an experiment on 2 resistance resistors of 100 and 200 ohms.

Calculations:

Determine the predictions of the equivalent resistance of the discharge system

    Series connection

  • Let U1 and U2 be the respective voltages on resistors R1 and R2, and let I be the current running through them
  • We know that U = U1 + U2
  • IR1 + IR2 = IRe1
  • R1 + R2 = Re1

    • Parallel connection

  • Let I1 and I2 be the respective currents through resistors R1 and R2, and let U be the voltage connected to them
  • We know that I1 + I2 = I
  • U/R1 + U/R2 = U/Re2
  • 1/R1 + 1/R2 = 1/Re2
  • Re2=(R1 + R2)/(R1R2)

  • Therefore, Re1 = 300 Ohms and Re2 = 150 Ohms.
